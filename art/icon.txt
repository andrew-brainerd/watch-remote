A square poster illustration of a single anthropomorphic TV remote control character, cel-shaded
anime style. This is a flat rectangular painting that completely covers a square canvas, edge to
edge, exactly like a cropped poster or album cover. It is NOT a badge, NOT a sticker, NOT a rounded
app tile — do not draw any icon-shaped container.

SUBJECT
A chunky, friendly TV remote — JUST THE HANDHELD DEVICE ITSELF, a floating object with no body, no
arms, no legs, no hands and no feet. Rounded-rectangle slab shape, generous chunky toy-like volume,
tilted on a lively diagonal of roughly 20 degrees and seen in a three-quarter view from the front, as
though hovering eagerly toward the viewer.

Its upper third is a large glowing rectangular SCREEN PANEL, noticeably lighter and brighter than the
dark body, and that screen is its face. Below the screen sits a tidy cluster of chunky oversized
buttons: one round directional pad and three or four large pill-shaped buttons. A short stubby
antenna rises from the top corner with a soft round glowing tip.

EXPRESSION — CRITICAL, THE WHOLE POINT
The character is EXCITED, delighted, joyful and full of fun — a burst of happy energy, like it just
heard great news. Both eyes are WIDE OPEN, big, round and sparkling, with large bright catchlights and
star-like glints of excitement. Both eyebrows are raised HIGH and symmetrically in cheerful surprise,
curved upward. A big, warm, open-mouthed happy smile — a broad joyful grin, teeth-free, mouth open in
delight. Two small soft blush marks on the cheeks of the screen. The whole face reads as beaming,
enthusiastic, playful and welcoming.

STYLE
Classic cel-shaded anime / modern TV-anime key-art rendering. Thick, confident, clean ink outlines of
even weight. FLAT color fills with hard-edged, sharply defined shadow shapes — two tones per surface
at most, no soft airbrush gradients on the character, no painterly texture, no visible brush strokes.
Bold rim light along the upper-left edge of the character. Crisp specular highlight shapes on the
plastic shell. High contrast, saturated, punchy, poster-like.

COMPOSITION
CRITICAL — THE FOUR CORNERS: the background glow must reach and completely fill all four corners of
the square canvas. The top-left, top-right, bottom-left and bottom-right corner pixels must all be
painted background color. There must be NO white anywhere in the image, and absolutely no rounded or
curved canvas corners. Picture the artwork continuing past the edge of the frame and being cropped
square.

The background must be a SMOOTH, CLEAN, UNIFORM radial gradient across the entire canvas, completely
undecorated. Do NOT place any ornament, motif, pattern, texture, blocks, tiles or shapes in or near
the corners — each corner is simply more of the same smooth gradient. The canvas edge is a plain
straight cut with no decoration whatsoever.

Perfectly square, full-bleed artwork that extends all the way to all four edges with no margin. Tight,
punchy, zoomed-in framing: the remote is centered and BIG, filling roughly 85% of the frame height and
75% of the frame width, so it dominates the composition. Only a slim band of background is visible
around it. Err on the side of too large rather than too small. Bold, instantly readable silhouette
that stays legible when scaled down to a tiny 40x40 pixel icon: few large shapes, no fine detail, no
thin lines, no small scattered elements, no busy patterning.
